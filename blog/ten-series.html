What is the Ten Series?

By Selena La Brooy • March 18, 2020

Over a series of 10 sessions, the practitioner and client work together to let go of poor postural habits and unwind holding patterns that cause the body strain and teach it to move, breathe and live in ways that are balanced, free and upright.

Dr. Rolf developed a systematic approach addressing key areas in each session. While unique to each body, overarching themes include:

Session 1

Opening the surface envelope of the body. Initial assessment of patterns and restrictions affecting breath capacity.

Session 2

Opening the feet and lower leg for mobilization and balance—the foundational platform.

Session 3

Releasing and opening the lateral line of the body.

Session 4

Releasing the mid-line, focusing on medial arch access and pelvic floor.

Session 5

Releasing deeper core, viscera and psoas; retraining functional support for motion.

Session 6

Releasing the backline fascia from eyebrows through head, back, legs to feet. Tension in one area may manifest elsewhere.

Session 7

Releasing and rebalancing head, neck and face fascia, including cranial work through nose and mouth.

Session 8

Organizing and retraining lower limbs to core.

Session 9

Organizing and retraining upper limbs to core.

Session 10

Horizontalizing and achieving whole body balance; anchoring all explorations and retraining.

Sessions may be split or combined with Rolf Movement sessions as needed. Post-series integration typically continues 6–12 months, with additional sessions recommended as life circumstances warrant.
